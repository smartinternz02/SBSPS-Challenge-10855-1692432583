Analyzing Agriculture in India and taking strategic decisions based on it

India is faced with the challenge of feeding a growing population while also addressing such risks as climate change, pandemics and supply chain disruptions. To address these challenges, the agricultural sector needs to become more agile and efficient.

There are currently more than 1,000 agri-tech startups in India offering a range of tech-based solutions, including digital finance, micro-insurance, access to agricultural inputs, quality testing, traceability and market connect platforms. They have the potential to significantly contribute to improving productivity and sustainability, but fragmented technological infrastructure, high cost of operations, lack of access to data and limited technical expertise, hamper the scale of theseÂ technologies.

Data analytics can help farmers monitor and optimize various aspects of their farm operations, such as soil health, crop growth, irrigation, fertilization, pest control, and harvest. By using sensors, drones, satellites, and other devices to collect and analyze data from the field, farmers can make informed decisions based on real-time and historical data. For example, IBM provides actionable agronomic insights for farmers using AI and cloud computing 1. Microsoft also offers a platform called Azure FarmBeats that enables data-driven farming techniques using IoT and AI 2.
